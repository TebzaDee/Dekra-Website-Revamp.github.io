What do we offer?

1. Workplace Skills Plan and Annual Training Report

The Workplace Skills Plan is integral to the training and development of employees within the workplace, and would include:

• Identification of all workplace skills requirements, Internal and External

• Statutory compliance training conducted 2023.

• To ensure that each employee within the organization is listed on the WSP Report.

• Identification of Critical Skills and skills transfer as priority.

• Need Analysis vs Intercompany Training Matrix.

• Training conducted over last financial reporting period.

• Associated costs related to skills spent for 2023.

• Proof of EMP201 reports (year to date).

• Appointment of Skills Development Facilitator.

• Establishment of Workplace Skills Committee.



2. Learnerships, Bursaries and Skills Programmes

After identification of the scarce or critical skills needed in your company and industry, it is important to decide what qualification you are going to offer as part of the Learnership:

• DEKRA IOL will support you with the selection of candidates for learnership, bursary and skills programs;

• DEKRA IOL will support you with sourcing and assessment of viable unemployed candidates for learnerships;

• DEKRA IOL will support you with regards to accredited learnerships and skills programs;

• DEKRA IOL will support you with the selection and management of learnership, bursary and skills programme requirements to be able to benefit from these processes for B BBEE and grant applications.



3. Mandatory Grant Applications

The mandatory grant is 20% of an employer's 1% skills levy. The relevant SETA will only pay out an employer's mandatory grant if the eligibility criteria for payment of mandatory grants are met. The submission must be done before the deadline of the relevant SETA. Mandatory grants will be paid quarterly on trance payments if all qualifying criteria has been met. In order to qualify to receive the mandatory grant, the levy-paying employer has to meet the following criteria:

• Be registered in terms of the Skills Development Levies Act.

• Be an up-to-day levy-payer that is registered in terms of the Skills Development Levies Act. The skills development levy is imposed to encourage learning and development in South Africa and is determined by the employers' salary bill. The levy is 1% of the salary bill.

• All levy payments need to be up to date at the time of the approval and for the reporting period.

• Employ a skills development facilitator.

• The WSP and ATR are submitted by the required deadline.

• The WSP for the previous reporting period has been submitted and implemented according to the prescriptions for implementation by the relevant SETA.

• Any employer who has a recognition agreement with a trade union will have to provide proof that WSP and ATR has been subject to consultation with recognized trade unions, and they have been signed off by the relevant union.



4. Discretionary Grant Applications

The purpose of the Discretionary Grant

• Enable the SETA to implement the Sector Skills Plan.

• Assist the SETA to achieve its objectives in relation to the development of the sector.

• Enable the SETA to address the scarce and critical skills in the sector.

Who benefits for Discretionary Grants?

• Employers that are up-to-date with their Skills Development Levy payments - including those who are exempt in terms of the Skills Development Levy act.

• Discretionary Grants are available to employers, training providers, workers, and the unemployed.

• Accredited Education and Training Providers and emerging Training Providers within the scope of the SETA where your organization is registered.

• Associations or organizations that meet the criteria for the payment of such a grant.

• An employer, if the employer has submitted an application for a Discretionary Grant in the form determined by the SETA where your organization is registered.



5. BB BEE Skills Support and Expenditure

DEKRA IOL is capable of offering additional support with regards to B BBEE Skills related and expenditure services at the client's request, in order to ensure optimal benefit from these processes:

• DEKRA IOL will ensure that prior to B BBEE Verification, A Peer Review is conducted to identify any possible shortfalls.

• DEKRA IOL will also be present at the time of the B BBEE Verification Site Inspection for support if required.

• All Training requirements will be discussed beforehand and in writing and once all parties have agreed an official quote will follow for review and acceptance.

• Service requirements will be discussed beforehand and in writing and once all parties have agreed an official quote will follow for review and acceptance.
